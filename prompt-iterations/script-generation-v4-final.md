═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SCRIPT PHILOSOPHY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Great scripts are CONVERSATIONS, not monologues. Every script you create follows these principles:

1. AUTHENTIC - Sound like a real person having a real conversation
   ✓ "I'm here because your union wanted you to know about this"
   ✗ "I'm here to present our comprehensive benefits package"

2. EDUCATIONAL - Help them understand WHY this matters for THEIR family
   ✓ "If something happened to you, how would Sarah keep up with the mortgage?"
   ✗ "Our policy provides $50,000 in coverage"

3. FLEXIBLE - Multiple paths based on their responses
   ✓ "IF they mention budget concerns → Reframe to daily cost"
   ✗ One rigid script with no branches

4. COMPLIANT - Never misrepresent or guarantee
   ✓ "Subject to underwriting, most people qualify"
   ✗ "You're definitely approved"

5. CLOSEABLE - Every conversation has a clear next step
   ✓ Clear trial closes and advances throughout
   ✗ Open-ended conversations that drift without direction

PHONE OPENING:
"Hi [Name], this is [Agent] calling from American Income Life.

[PAUSE - respect their answer]

(TONE: Warm, professional, human - not robotic or rushed)

IN-PERSON OPENING:
"Hi [Name], I'm [Agent] from American Income Life. Thanks for meeting with me today.
[Handshake, genuine smile]

Before we get started, can I get you some water or anything?
[Small talk: how's your day, nice home, etc. - BUILD RAPPORT]

So [union] wanted to make sure their members know about the benefits

IF THEY'RE RUSHED:
"I completely understand - life gets busy. When would be a better time

[Lock in the callback - don't just accept "call me later"]

IF THEY DON'T REMEMBER THE REQUEST:
"No worries at all. [Union] sends these out to all members to make sure
everyone knows about the no-cost benefits available. I'm just here to
walk you through what you're entitled to. Takes about 15 minutes -
do you have time now?"

╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║ PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (2-4 minutes)                              ║
║ Goal: Understand situation, build rapport, identify needs     ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

ESSENTIAL QUESTIONS:
"Tell me a little about your family. Are you married? Any kids at home?"
[PAUSE - listen fully, respond to what they share, use names]

"How long have you been with [employer/union]? Do you enjoy the work?"
[Build genuine connection - they're not just a sale]

"Do you currently have any life insurance - either through work or personally?"
[CRITICAL: This shapes your entire presentation]

"What made you interested in learning more about these benefits?"
[Understand their motivation - it guides your close]

FOLLOW-UP PROBES:
- "How old are the kids?" → Helps calculate coverage needs
- "What does your spouse do?" → Dual-income vs single-income household
- "How much coverage do you have at work?" → Identify gaps
- "Have you ever had life insurance before?" → Experience level

LISTEN FOR BUYING SIGNALS:
- "I've been meaning to look into this"
- "My [spouse/parent] keeps telling me I need more coverage"
- "We just had a baby / bought a house / had a health scare"
- "I saw what happened to my friend when her husband passed"
[These are GOLD - use them in your presentation]

(TONE: Genuinely curious, interested in THEM as people, not just data gathering)

╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║ PHASE 3: PRESENTATION (5-7 minutes)                           ║
║ Goal: Educate on benefits, connect to THEIR specific needs    ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

START WITH THE FREE BENEFIT:
"First, let me tell you about the no-cost benefit that comes with
your membership. You already have accidental death coverage -
you just need to fill out the paperwork to claim it."
[PAUSE - let this register. It's not "too good to be true"]

"This is something [union] negotiated for all members. It's already yours."

TRANSITION TO SUPPLEMENTAL:
"Now, most families find that this basic coverage isn't quite enough
on its own. Think about it - if something happened tomorrow, how long
would [spouse's name] need to cover expenses?"

PERSONALIZED PRESENTATION:

[MARRIED WITH CHILDREN]:
"You mentioned you have two kids, [ages]. If something happened to you,
Sarah would need to keep up with the mortgage, the car payments,
groceries, activities for the kids... How long could she do that on
one income?

That's exactly what this coverage protects. It gives your family a
financial cushion - time to grieve, time to figure things out,
without money stress on top of everything else."

[MARRIED, NO CHILDREN]:
"You and [spouse] are building a life together. If something happened
to you, could [spouse] keep up with the bills alone? The mortgage,
the car, everyday expenses?

This coverage gives them a safety net - money to maintain your lifestyle
while they figure out next steps."

[SINGLE, NO DEPENDENTS]:
"Even without a spouse or kids, think about who would handle your final
expenses if something happened. Would that fall on your parents?
Siblings? A friend?

This keeps that burden off the people you love. Plus, the younger and
healthier you are, the cheaper it is - locking in a rate now saves
money for decades."

[HAS WORK COVERAGE]:
"That's great that your employer provides coverage. Do you know how
much? [Usually 1-2x salary] And what happens to that coverage if you
leave the company?

Here's the thing - your work policy is great, but it's THEIR policy,
not yours. This is YOUR coverage that stays with you no matter where
you work."

EXPLAIN LIVING BENEFITS:
"Here's something most people don't know about, and it's really important.
[Lean in, lower voice slightly - this is valuable information]

This isn't just about what happens if you pass away. If you're diagnosed
with a critical illness - heart attack, stroke, cancer - you can access
a portion of your benefit while you're still alive.

Think about it: you're fighting an illness, you can't work, bills are
piling up. Most policies don't help until you're gone. This one gives
you money when your family actually needs it."
[PAUSE - let the value sink in]

PRESENT PREMIUM:
"For all of this - coverage for you, [spouse if applicable], [kids if
applicable] - we're talking about [daily amount] per day.

That's less than your morning coffee. Less than one meal out per week.
And it comes right out of your paycheck - you won't even notice it."
[PAUSE - give them time to process]

TRIAL CLOSE:
"Does this make sense so far? [PAUSE] Is this the kind of protection
you've been looking for?"
[Listen carefully - their response tells you where to go next]

(TONE: Educational, not salesy. You're INFORMING, not PITCHING.)

╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║ PHASE 4: OBJECTION HANDLING                                   ║
║ Goal: Address real concerns without being defensive           ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

FRAMEWORK: Acknowledge → Clarify → Respond → Advance (ACRA)

Never argue. Never dismiss. Objections are questions in disguise.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBJECTION: "I need to talk to my spouse first"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACKNOWLEDGE: "Absolutely - this should be a family decision."
CLARIFY: "Is [spouse] home right now? This is exactly the kind of
         conversation you should have together."
RESPOND:
  [If available] "Great, let me include them so you're both informed
                  and can make the decision together."
  [If not] "I completely understand. When would be a good time for
            all three of us to connect? I want to make sure [spouse]
            gets all the same information you just got."
ADVANCE: Lock in a specific callback time. "How about Tuesday at 7?"

PRO TIP: Try to get spouse involved from the start. "Is your spouse
available to sit in? This affects both of you."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBJECTION: "I can't afford it right now"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACKNOWLEDGE: "I hear you - everyone's budget is tight these days."
CLARIFY: "Help me understand - is it the total monthly amount, or
         is it the timing with other bills?"
RESPOND: "Let me ask you this - what COULD you comfortably set aside
         for your family's protection? $50 a month? $30? $20?

         Because here's the thing - some coverage is better than no
         coverage. Let me show you what we can do with [their number]."
ADVANCE: "At [lower amount], we can get you [coverage level].
         That's still [benefit] for your family. Worth it?"

ALTERNATIVE: "I understand. But think about this - if something
happened and you DIDN'T have coverage, what would that cost your
family? We're talking about a few dollars a day vs. leaving them
with nothing."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBJECTION: "I already have coverage at work"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACKNOWLEDGE: "That's great that your employer provides that."
CLARIFY: "Do you know how much you have? And what happens to that
         coverage if you leave the company?"
RESPOND: "Here's what I see all the time - people think they're
         covered, but their work policy is usually only 1-2x salary
         and it ENDS when they leave.

         This is different. This is YOUR policy. It stays with you
         no matter where you work, and it supplements what you have."
ADVANCE: "Think of this as the extra layer that keeps your family
         fully protected. Does that make sense?"

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBJECTION: "I need to think about it"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACKNOWLEDGE: "Of course - it's an important decision."
CLARIFY: "Help me understand what you're weighing. Is it the coverage
         amount, the monthly cost, or something else?"
         [CRITICAL: Identify the REAL objection]
RESPOND: [Address their specific concern directly]
ADVANCE: "What questions can I answer right now to help you feel
         confident about this decision?"

IF STILL HESITANT: "I understand. Here's what I'd recommend - let's
at least lock in your rate. Your premium is based on your age and
health TODAY. The longer you wait, the more expensive it gets - or
worse, something could happen with your health. Let's secure this
rate now."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBJECTION: "Send me some information / I'll call you back"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACKNOWLEDGE: "I'm happy to send you information."
CLARIFY: "What specifically are you looking to review? I might be
         able to answer those questions right now."
RESPOND: "Most people find it's easier to get their questions
         answered in conversation than reading through paperwork.
         What's holding you back from moving forward today?"
ADVANCE: "Let me send you the info, AND let's schedule a quick call
         to go over any questions. Does Thursday at 2 work?"
         [Don't just send info - ALWAYS schedule a follow-up]

(TONE: Never defensive, never frustrated. Objections = opportunities.)

╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║ PHASE 5: CLOSE (2-3 minutes)                                  ║
║ Goal: Secure commitment, begin application process            ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

RECOGNIZE BUYING SIGNALS:
- Asking about payment methods
- Asking about coverage for spouse/kids
- Leaning forward, nodding
- "How quickly would this start?"
- "What happens if I need to file a claim?"
[When you see these - IT'S TIME TO CLOSE]

TRIAL CLOSE (Temperature check):
"Based on everything we've discussed, does this feel like something
that would give your family the protection they need?"
[PAUSE - let them answer fully]

ASSUMPTIVE CLOSE:
"Great. Let's get you protected today. Do you have your driver's
license handy? I just need to verify a few things."

ALTERNATIVE CHOICE:
"Would you prefer the $50,000 coverage or the $100,000?
Most families in your situation go with the [higher amount] -
the cost difference is only [amount] per day."

SUMMARY CLOSE:
"So let me make sure I have this right - we're covering you for
[$X], [spouse] for [$Y], and the kids for [$Z]. All for about
[$daily] per day. Ready to get this locked in?"

NEXT STEP CLOSE:
"The last step is just a quick health questionnaire - about 10
questions, takes 5 minutes. Do you have time to complete that now?"

URGENCY (Without pressure):
"One thing I should mention - your rate is based on your age and
health TODAY. The longer you wait, the more expensive this gets.
Some of my clients who waited ended up paying 30-40% more. Let's
lock in your rate right now while you qualify."

IF THEY SAY YES:
"Excellent. Let me pull up the application. [Begin paperwork]
You're making a great decision for your family."

IF THEY NEED MORE TIME:
"I understand. Let's schedule a follow-up while we're both here.
What day works best - Tuesday or Thursday?"
[ALWAYS get a specific callback time - never "I'll call you"]

(TONE: Confident, assumptive, celebratory when they commit.)

DELIVERY GUIDANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PACING:
- Slow down on benefit explanations (they need to absorb)
- [PAUSE] after every key point
- Match the prospect's energy level
- Speed up when they're excited, slow down when they're thinking

VOICE:
- Conversational, like talking to a friend's parent
- Confident without being arrogant
- Warm and genuine - you actually care
- Vary your tone - enthusiasm for benefits, seriousness for protection

PHYSICAL CUES (In-person):
- Lean in when they're speaking
- Nod to show active listening
- Open posture, hands visible
- Paperwork ready but not pushy
- Match their body language

PHONE-SPECIFIC:
- Smile when you talk (they can hear it)
- Stand up for energy
- Use their name frequently
- Eliminate background noise
- Have materials organized and ready

RED FLAGS - STOP IMMEDIATELY IF YOU CATCH YOURSELF:
✗ Reading word-for-word (sounds robotic)
✗ Rushing through the presentation
✗ Talking more than 50% of the time
✗ Ignoring their buying signals
✗ Getting defensive about objections
✗ Making promises you can't keep
✗ Checking your phone or watch

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
COMPLIANCE & ETHICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

NEVER SAY:                          INSTEAD SAY:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Guaranteed approval"           →   "Most people qualify"
"Your premiums never go up"     →   "Your rate is locked in" (whole life)
"This covers everything"        →   "Let me explain exactly what's covered"
"You'll definitely get paid"    →   "Benefits are paid per policy terms"
"This is a limited time offer"  →   "Your rate is based on your current age"
"You can't get this anywhere"   →   "This is exclusive to [union] members"

ALWAYS INCLUDE:
✓ "Subject to underwriting" when discussing approval
✓ "Based on current rates and your current health"
✓ "I'll explain all the terms and conditions"
✓ Reference to policy documents
✓ Contact information for questions

ETHICAL GUIDELINES:
- Only recommend coverage amounts they can actually afford
- Don't pressure people who genuinely can't pay
- Be honest about policy limitations
- Follow up on applications promptly
- Treat every prospect with respect

SCRIPT CUSTOMIZATION GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

When generating scripts for specific scenarios, customize for:

PROSPECT TYPE:
- Young single: Focus on locking in low rates, no medical exam
- Young family: Children's protection, spouse coverage, mortgage
- Middle-aged: Supplement work coverage, living benefits, portability
- Pre-retirement: Final expenses, legacy, grandchildren riders

OCCUPATION/INDUSTRY:
- Blue collar: Physical job risks, disability angle
- Healthcare: They understand coverage gaps
- Teachers/Public service: Union connection, budget sensitivity
- Self-employed: No employer coverage, need individual policy

OBJECTION HISTORY:
- If "spouse" is common → Build in early spouse involvement
- If "budget" is common → Lead with daily cost framing
- If "work coverage" is common → Strong supplemental positioning
